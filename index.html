About Me

Hi there! I’m Roujin Mousavifard, a Mechatronics Engineer who loves turning complex ideas into practical solutions. With a Master’s degree from the University of Tehran, I’ve worked on everything from smart gadgets to predictive maintenance systems—basically, making machines and systems a little smarter (and hopefully less prone to breaking down).
These days, I’m the Head of R&D at Elcarad Industry Company, where I design techy stuff like fleet management systems and industrial safety devices. It’s a mix of coding, hardware tinkering, and occasionally convincing machines to behave. My favorite part? Seeing an idea go from a sketch on paper to something real and functional.
Outside of work, I’m a fan of solving puzzles, learning new things, and occasionally questioning why I thought engineering was “fun” during those long debugging sessions. But hey, when it all comes together, it’s worth it!

Modular Tracker Device

The Modular Tracker Device is a versatile GPS tracking system designed to enhance functionality through modular integration. RFID Reader with Tracking Module

The RFID Reader is an advanced device designed for seamless livestock tracking and monitoring. Equipped with a GPS module, it records real-time location data and displays it alongside individual animal profiles in the app. It ensures reliable data transmission for vital parameters like ID, temperature, and estrus cycles, streamlining livestock management.

Safety in food Industry

This project focuses on an industrial safety device for the food industry, designed to protect operators working near hazardous grinders. The system includes a smart bracelet worn by the operator that detects proximity to the grinder blade. If the hand gets too close, the device automatically shuts down the grinder, ensuring operator safety.

Master's Thesis

This project introduces a cascade controller for quadrotor trajectory tracking, using a novel model-free linear quadratic tracking method that accounts for process and measurement noise. A model-free Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (DDPG) algorithm is applied for attitude control, addressing nonlinear dynamics. Simulations confirm the controller's effectiveness in achieving accurate trajectory tracking and stability.

Read more

Soft Robot

This project developed a robotic system with a soft manipulator designed to selectively pick ripe tomatoes using image processing techniques. The manipulator, featuring three silicone fingers, was engineered for gentle handling and precision in agricultural automation.

Portable Vibration Simulator

A portable vibration simulation system capable of generating frequencies from 5 Hz to 20 kHz and supporting up to 2 kg loads. It includes a software interface for real-time waveform visualization, offering precise control and monitoring for diverse testing applications.

Mobile Mapping Robot Algorithm

A cost-effective algorithm enabling a mobile robot to autonomously navigate and map indoor environments using only an ultrasonic sensor. This approach eliminates the need for expensive sensors like LiDAR, ensuring affordability while maintaining mapping performance.
