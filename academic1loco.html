Loco-Manipulation on Kangaroo & Unitree G1

This humanoid loco-manipulation project develops a hybrid control framework that investigates and compares Model Predictive Control (MPC) and the Divergent Component of Motion (DCM) for locomotion, while leveraging Reinforcement Learning (RL) to enable the Unitree-G1 and Kangaroo humanoid robots to perform dexterous manipulation during walking. In the DCM-based branch, the controller regulates the unstable component of the center-of-mass (CoM) dynamics and computes desired wrenches that are distributed to all joints via inverse dynamics within a whole-body controller. In the MPC-based branch, the planner generates feasible trajectories for the center of mass, momentum, and contact forces. RL supplies residual policies for grasping and object interaction to improve robustness and adaptability. The system is implemented in MATLAB/Simulink, ROS, and MuJoCo, with training and evaluation performed on GPU-accelerated hardware.
